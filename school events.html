SHIVAJI MAHARAJ JAYANTI 2023

“Valor is strength, not of legs and arms, but of heart and soul; it consists not in the worth of our horse or our weapons, but in our own.” This quote truly states what Shivaji was and remembered for. On 19th February 2023, Sanskar Vidya Sagar, celebrated Shivaji Jayanti the Birth anniversary of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaja. Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj is today considered a national hero in India and especially in the state of Maharashtra. The Programme started with lightning of lamp by Principal Hemalatha Ma'am with a prayer song, followed by national anthem. Atul Sir was the comparer and gave information about Shivaji Maharaja his family, mother and also being master of the guerrilla warfare. Speeches in English, Marathi and Sanskrit we given by students highlighting Chhatrapati's success, valour and courage. A famous powada(depicting a courageous act of Shivaji) was presented. Hemalatha Ma'am in her address stressed upon imbibing the leadership skills, his insight, foresightedness bravery all need to be taken as a lesson from the leader. The programme ended with national song.

REPORT ON CBSE SCIENCE EXHIBITION

“Innovation is seeing what everybody has seen and thinking what nobody has thought” Learning and innovation go hand in hand. Learning is a process which is continuous and never exhaust the mind. In this creativity is thinking up new things , with this notion Sanskar Vidya Sagar participated in CBSE Science Exhibition conducted by CBSE every year. This year the theme was “Technology and Toys” and the school participated under the sub ⁷theme of “historical development with current innovation”. Students participated in both senior and junior categories with their innovative exhibits, conflation of technology and scientific knowledge to generate a toy to give out concepts of various subjects in a playway method. The students participated with their exhibits of Gyan Chaupaddh in senior category and Lakshya in the junior category. There were all together 71 exhibits from 44 schools in the regional level . In the junior category Mst. Sarthak and Mst. Divyanshu were selected for the National level of the exhibition. Their exhibit Lakshya imparts the concept of laws of reflection through the ancient act of Arjuna aiming at the fish’s eye, while looking only at its reflection in the water below. All the participants were acknowledged for their efforts with a participation certificate presented to them by Mr Rajesh Kumar Gupta Assistant Secretary CBSE (Pune region) It is a proud moment for the school and another feather added to the cap. The School Director Mrs Asha Kamath and Principal Mrs Hemlatha Ambadwar congratulation the students and teacher incharge Mrs Shweta Deshpande and Mr. Sumit Mahatpure. They urged others to be a part of such activities which is always a learning experience.
